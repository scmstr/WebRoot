Good Web Resources to Help App Developers


W3Schools
W3Schools is a site that comes directly from the W3 organization. They have beginner-friendly pages for almost all major languages, and each tool, method, function, and idea for all web pages and design. One of the most informative sources go directly into writing code.

LinkedIn Learning
LinkedIn Learning is a great source to deeply understand an idea and apply it to modern business practices and workflows. Their courses start at the very beginning of a subject, and with cleanly and logically subdivided video sections walking you through tons of major subjects, and more and more professional content being added and updated daily. LinkedIn Learning is a membership-subscribe premium service with previews and options for students.

Google Images
Google Images also will come as a surprise to a lot of people, but having a keyword searched grid of images by one of the most sophisticated search algorithms in the world can be pretty helpful, and upon quickly scanning across a quick search result, can be the difference between hours of videos or reading, or simply a few seconds that it takes for a good or new visual to understand how an idea works. Never underestimate either the power of actually seeing it to understand, nor the power of human convolution in words to waste time.

Sometimes, we have to suck it up and actually refer to the official documentation of a language. Most languages and systems are either old enough and well enough used to have great official documentation or be new enough to understand the importance of concise, accurate documentation. The information is there, you just have to reach out and actually read it.
